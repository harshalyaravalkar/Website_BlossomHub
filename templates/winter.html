Winter Flowers

Common Name: Pansy
Scientific Name: Viola × wittrockiana
In India, pansies (Viola × wittrockiana) are most common in late fall and early spring in the northern areas. In subtropical areas, they bloom all winter.

Common Name: Aster
Scientific name: Aster amellus
In North India, aster seeds can be sown between June and September, or January and February. Flowers start appearing 60 days after sowing and the blooming period lasts for about 15-20 days.

Common Name: Kunda, Downy Jasmine
Scientific Name: Jasminum multiflorum
Kunda, also known as downy jasmine, is a popular landscape plant that blooms nearly year-round. Kunda plants can bloom in the winter. You can grow kunda plants from July-August onwards.

Common Name: Dahlia
Scientific Name: Dahlia pinnata
The dahlia growing season in India is from mid-January to April. Dahlias are winter plants that can't tolerate hot summers. They begin flowering within 90-100 days of planting and can bloom for up to 120 days.
